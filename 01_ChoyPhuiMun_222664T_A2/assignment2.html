What are Tectonic Plates?

Before we dive into the world of Tectonic Plates, we first need to know what makes up the Earth.

The Earth is made out of four major layers. staring from the topmost layer: the Crust, Mantle, Outer Core and the Inner Core being the deepest layer.

EARTH LAYERS

Let's learn about the layers of the Earth.

THE CRUST

It mostly consist of soil and rocks

The crust about 5 to 70 kilometers thick, which consist two kinds or crust:

  • Oceanic Crust - about 5 - 10 kilometers thick & found under ocean bodies
  • Continental Crust - about 30 - 70 kilometers thick & is the ground that we walk on

THE MANTLE

It is a thick solid substance that takes up about 85% of the total mass of the earth.

The mantle is about 2,900 kilometers thick, and it's the thickest layer of the planet.

This layer is divided into to layers:

  • Upper Mantle - about 720 kilometers thick
  • Lower Mantle - about 2171 kilometers thick

THE OUTER CORE

It is a fluid layer about 2,400 kilometers and consist of mostly iron and nickel

THE INNER CORE

It is a solid ball with a radius of about 1,220 kilometers and consist of iron and nickel

NEXT

Tectonic Plates consist of the Earth's outermost layer, or called lithosphere.

Lithosphere is made up of the crust and the upper mantle - which is broken up into large plates called tectonic plates.

Due to the plates lying on the Asthenosphere (partially molten layer of rock), convection between these planes would cause the tectonic plates to move about 2 to 15 centimeters relative to each other per year.

Types of  Plate Boundaries

Plate Boundaries are zones that where an lithospheric plate meent another lithospheric plate and they are borders of plates. At these plate Boundaries, plates would interact with each other due to stress accumulated and released at these zones, which causes them to move

The movement in these plate boundaries can be categorised to three types of Plate Boundaries: Convergent, Divergent and Transform boudaries.

DIVERGENT PLATE BOUNDARIES

This is where two plates slide apart from each other.

When an oceanic plate slides away from another oceanic plate, an ocean basin is formed.

Examples of Oceanic-to-Oceanic Ocean Basins:

  • Mid-Atlantic Ridge
  • East Pacific Rise

When an continental plate moves away from another continetal plate, a new ocean basin is formed and ocean to fill it up.

Examples of Continent-to-Continent Ocean Basins:

  • West Antarctic Rift System
  • East African Rift

CONVERGENT PLATE BOUNDARIES

This is where two plates slide toward each other, which the denser plate slips beneath the less dense plate.

When a denser and older oceanic plate slides toward another less dense oceanic plate, volcanic islands and trenches are formed.

Examples of Oceanic-to-Oceanic Volcanic Islands:

  • Mariana Islands
  • Aleutian Islands

When a denser oceanic plate slides toward another less dense continetal plate, subduction mountain ranges are formed.

Examples of Continent-to-Continent Mountain Ranges:

  • Andes
  • Cascade Range

TRANFORM PLATE BOUNDARIES

This is where two plates slide past each other along transform faults.

Example of Transform Fault:

  • San Andreas Fault

Issues caused by  Tectonic Plates
EARTHQUAKES

Earthquakes is the shaking of the surface which is caused by a sudden release of stored energy in rocks found along fault line (transform plate boundaries).

This sudden movement of the ground could lead to destruction that can impact us human. These are the negative effects of earthquakes to humans.

Loss Of Lives

As it is an unexpected event, peopel would not be prepared or have enought time to evacuate safely. Thus, many people would lose thier lives due the earthquakes.

In order to prevent from losing lives, places which are more prove to earthquakes should have their Government or Non-Government Organisations (NGOs) to implement evacuation drills to train their residents what should they do when an earthquake starts. This would increase the level of prepareness of those affected.

Destruction of Infrastructure

Some building are not earthquake-proof due to it being old or not have stable fondation. These builings are dangerous during the earthquake as it not strong enough to withstand the shaking of the ground and can be easily destroyed. It is alo dangerous for residents to be near to these kinds of building as it might injure or kill people near it once it get destroyed and crumbled.

The government of these places that are prone of earthquakes could implement regular checks on buildinga to make sure that in th case of emergency,the buildig is stable and safe enough for residents to take shelter.
